Writing

Essay · Evolutionary Biology & Policy

How can evolutionary biology inform investing for systems change?

An original essay applying evolutionary mismatch theory to the practice of systems change investment — arguing that strategies succeed when they work with evolved human psychology rather than against it. Uses TWIST case studies including Regen Melbourne and Pioneers of Our Time.

Research

MPhil Dissertation · Cambridge University

Reproductive Patterns and Inflammation: An Evolutionary Mismatch Perspective

Using data from the 1946 NSHD birth cohort to examine whether cumulative reproductive cycling exposure is associated with IBS risk in women — applying an evolutionary mismatch framework to women's gastrointestinal health. Supervised by Dr Emma Pomeroy, Dept of Archaeology.

About

Evolutionary science, applied to the real world

I'm an MPhil student at Cambridge studying Human Evolutionary Studies, with research focused on women's reproductive health and evolutionary medicine. I care about getting rigorous science out of academic journals and into conversations that change how people understand their own bodies.

Alongside my research, I host Ancient Bodies on CamFM and lead events and outreach for Cambridge Femtech Society. Previously, I studied Human Sciences at UCL (First Class Honours), worked in Mallorca as a British Council English Language Assistant, and interned in a stealth-stage health tech startup.
